<p><p>The EV charging equipment market is primarily divided into two types: DC charging and AC charging. DC charging, or direct current charging, delivers high power directly to the vehicle's battery, enabling rapid charging times, often within 30 minutes. This makes it ideal for fast charging stations on highways. AC charging, or alternating current charging, connects to the vehicle’s onboard charger, typically providing lower power levels, suitable for home installations and slower public chargers, taking several hours for a full charge.</p></p>
