<p><p>An OBD2 (On-Board Diagnostics II) scanner is a diagnostic tool used to interface with a vehicle's onboard computer system. It reads and interprets diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) generated by the vehicle's sensors, helping users identify issues related to the engine, transmission, emissions, and other systems. OBD2 scanners are widely used by automotive professionals, fleet managers, and individual car owners for maintenance, troubleshooting, and emissions testing.</p><p>The OBD2 Scanner Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing vehicle complexity, stricter emission regulations, and the rising demand for advanced diagnostic tools. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.1% during the forecast period. Additionally, the rise of electric and hybrid vehicles is creating new opportunities for advanced OBD2 scanners capable of handling unique diagnostic requirements. As automotive technology evolves, the OBD2 scanner market is poised for continued expansion, catering to both professional and consumer needs.</p></p>
