<p><p>The Japanese Encephalitis Virus Vaccine market primarily consists of two types: inactivated vaccines and live attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines contain virus particles that have been killed, providing safe immunity without risk of disease, making them suitable for vulnerable populations. In contrast, live attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of the virus to stimulate a stronger immune response, often requiring fewer doses. Both types play crucial roles in controlling Japanese encephalitis, particularly in endemic regions and for travelers.</p></p>
