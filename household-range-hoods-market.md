<p><p>Household range hoods are categorized into two primary types based on their functionality and installation: Type I and Type II. Type I hoods are designed for cooking appliances that produce grease and smoke, typically equipped with filters to remove contaminants and ensure proper ventilation. They are commonly used over stoves. Type II hoods, on the other hand, are focused on ventilating non-greasy cooking processes, like steaming or boiling, and are less complex, often lacking grease filters. Both types enhance indoor air quality in kitchens.</p></p>
