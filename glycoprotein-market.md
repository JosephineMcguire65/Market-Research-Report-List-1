<p><p>The glycoprotein market is categorized primarily into N-linked and O-linked types, along with other diverse categories. N-linked glycoproteins feature carbohydrates attached to nitrogen atoms in asparagine residues, commonly found in cellular structures and immune response mechanisms. O-linked glycoproteins, on the other hand, have sugars linked to oxygen atoms in serine or threonine residues, playing roles in cell signaling and recognition. Other markets may include less common glycosylation types or modified structures, catering to specialized applications in biotechnology and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
