<p><p>The remote airborne particle counters market is divided into two main types: ionizing and non-ionizing. Ionizing counters utilize radiation to detect and measure particles, offering high sensitivity and accuracy, making them suitable for environments with stringent monitoring requirements. In contrast, non-ionizing counters rely on optical or laser technologies to identify airborne particles without radiation, making them safer for various applications. Both types cater to diverse industries, including pharmaceuticals, cleanrooms, and environmental monitoring, each chosen based on specific detection needs and regulatory standards.</p></p>
