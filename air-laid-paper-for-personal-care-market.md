<p><p>Air laid paper is a non-woven fabric made from pulp fibers, commonly used in personal care products like diapers and feminine hygiene items. The market for air laid paper operates primarily through two bonding types: chemical bonding and thermal bonding. Chemical bonding involves the use of adhesives or binders to create strong fiber networks, enhancing durability and absorbency. In contrast, thermal bonding utilizes heat to fuse fibers together, offering a cost-effective, lightweight option with good structural integrity, making it ideal for various personal care applications.</p></p>
