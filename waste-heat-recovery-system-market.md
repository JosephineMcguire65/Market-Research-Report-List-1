<p><p>The Waste Heat Recovery System market includes two primary types: Steam Rankine Cycle (SRC) and Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC). SRC utilizes high-temperature waste heat to produce steam, which drives a turbine for electricity generation, commonly used in industries with substantial thermal output. ORC, on the other hand, employs organic fluids with lower boiling points, enabling energy recovery from lower temperature waste heat sources. Both technologies aim to enhance energy efficiency and reduce environmental impact by converting waste heat into usable energy.</p></p>
