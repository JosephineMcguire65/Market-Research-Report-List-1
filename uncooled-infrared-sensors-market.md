<p><p>Uncooled infrared sensors are divided into two primary market types: thermal and quantum. Thermal sensors detect infrared radiation based on temperature changes, relying on materials like microbolometers. They are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and reliability in various applications like surveillance and automotive. Quantum sensors, on the other hand, exploit the photonic properties of materials to achieve higher sensitivity and resolution, making them ideal for advanced applications such as medical imaging and military surveillance. Both markets are expanding due to increasing demand for thermal imaging technologies.</p></p>
