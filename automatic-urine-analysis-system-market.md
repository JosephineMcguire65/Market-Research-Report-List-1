<p><p>The Automatic Urine Analysis System market is primarily divided into two types: dry analysis and wet analysis. Dry analysis involves the use of test strips that are chemically treated to react with specific components in urine, providing rapid results without the need for liquid reagents. In contrast, wet analysis involves the use of traditional laboratory methods requiring liquid reagents, typically yielding more comprehensive biochemical information. Each method addresses different clinical needs, influencing their adoption in healthcare facilities.</p></p>
