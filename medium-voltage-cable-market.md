<p><p>The medium voltage cable market comprises three primary types based on conductor materials: copper, aluminum, and aluminum alloy. Copper cables are favored for their excellent conductivity and durability, making them suitable for high-performance applications. Aluminum cables are lighter and more cost-effective, often used in overhead installations. Aluminum alloy cables combine the advantages of both materials, offering enhanced strength and conductivity while being lighter than pure copper. Each type serves specific needs in electrical distribution systems, balancing performance and cost.</p></p>
