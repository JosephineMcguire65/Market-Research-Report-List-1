<p><p>Medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs) are a type of fat composed of medium-length fatty acid chains, typically containing 6 to 12 carbon atoms. They are commonly derived from coconut oil, palm kernel oil, and dairy products. MCTs are easily digestible and rapidly absorbed by the body, making them a popular ingredient in dietary supplements, sports nutrition, and functional foods. They are also used in medical nutrition for patients with malabsorption issues and in ketogenic diets due to their ability to promote ketone production.</p><p>The Medium-chain Triglycerides Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing consumer awareness of health and wellness, rising demand for functional foods, and the growing popularity of ketogenic and low-carb diets. The Asia-Pacific region is emerging as a high-growth market due to increasing disposable incomes and a growing focus on health and nutrition.</p></p>
