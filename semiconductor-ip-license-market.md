<p><p>The semiconductor IP license market comprises three primary types: soft cores, hard cores, and firm cores. Soft cores are flexible, synthesizable designs that can be customized for various applications. Hard cores are fixed, optimized hardware implementations, providing high performance but less flexibility. Firm cores strike a balance between the two, offering a mostly fixed architecture with some configurable options. These core types cater to different design needs and performance requirements across the semiconductor industry, enabling tailored solutions for diverse products.</p></p>
