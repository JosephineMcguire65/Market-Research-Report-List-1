<p><p>Flash chromatography is a rapid purification technique used to separate compounds based on their chemical properties. The market is segmented into various types:</p><p>1. **Reverse Phase**: Utilizes a non-polar stationary phase and polar mobile phase, ideal for separating non-polar compounds.</p><p>2. **Ion Exchange**: Separates molecules based on charge using charged stationary phases, effective for proteins and nucleic acids.</p><p>3. **Size Exclusion**: Separates by molecular size, with larger molecules eluting first.</p><p>4. **Chiral Separation**: Isolates enantiomers using chiral stationary phases.</p><p>5. **Others**: Includes affinity chromatography and normal phase, catering to diverse separation needs. Each type serves specific applications in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and research.</p></p>
