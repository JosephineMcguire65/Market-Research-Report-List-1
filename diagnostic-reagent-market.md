<p><p>The diagnostic reagent market is divided into two primary types: in vivo and in vitro diagnostic reagents. In vivo diagnostic reagents are substances used within the body to diagnose diseases, often through imaging or biochemical tests. In contrast, in vitro diagnostic reagents are utilized outside the body, testing specimens like blood or tissues in laboratories for disease identification and management. Both types are crucial for accurate medical diagnostics, guiding treatment decisions and enhancing patient care.</p></p>
