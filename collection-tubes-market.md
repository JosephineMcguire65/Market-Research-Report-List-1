<p><p>The collection tubes market primarily consists of two types: glass and PET (polyethylene terephthalate). Glass tubes are favored for their inert properties, ensuring sample integrity and compatibility with various substances, making them ideal for clinical and laboratory uses. Conversely, PET tubes are lightweight, shatter-resistant, and often more cost-effective, appealing to facilities prioritizing safety and convenience. Both types cater to different needs, with glass dominating high-precision applications and PET gaining traction in routine testing and larger-scale collection scenarios.</p></p>
