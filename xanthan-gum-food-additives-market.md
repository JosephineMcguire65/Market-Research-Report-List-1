<p><p>Xanthan gum is a polysaccharide used as a food additive, primarily as a thickening, stabilizing, and emulsifying agent. It is produced through the fermentation of sugars by the bacterium *Xanthomonas campestris*. Commonly found in products like salad dressings, sauces, dairy alternatives, and gluten-free baked goods, xanthan gum enhances texture, shelf life, and consistency. Its versatility and compatibility with various food formulations make it a popular choice in the food industry.</p><p>The Xanthan Gum Food Additives Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.1% during the forecast period. The Asia-Pacific region is emerging as a key market due to rapid urbanization, changing dietary habits, and the expansion of the food and beverage sector.
